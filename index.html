Research interest

My research is mainly focused on the use of mathematical tools to better understand the transmission of pathogens in humans and animals as well as their diffusion in
the environment with a One Health approach.

Curriculum vitæ

Education

  • 2017-2019 : Master's degree in ecological modelling (Université de Rennes 1, Rennes, France)

  • 2014-2017 : Bachelor's degree in economic analysis (Université Paris II - Panthéon-Assas, Paris, France)
Research Experience
  • Sept. 2024 - Sept. 2026: Research fellow in epidemiology, INRAE, Marcy-l'étoile, France
    My role is to build a tool for real-time investigation of disease transmission on the French cattle network.

  • Sept. 2020 - Nov. 2023: PhD in Public Health, Conservatoire national des arts et métiers, Paris, France
    Title : "Modelling the transmission of HCV in hospital settings : from assessment to control, an application to the Egyptian context"

  • Sept. 2019 - Sept. 2020 : Modelling engineer, Inserm - Institut Pasteur, Paris, France
    My role was to help understand the impact of the eco-exposome on the antiobiotic resistance dynamics in wastewater through dynamic modelling.
    I built a a mechanistic model and informed its parameters with data collected in France (PANDORE project) using MCMC techniques.

  • Jan. 2019 - June 2019 : Research intern, Conservatoire national des arts et métiers, Paris, France
    During my second internship at the Cnam I extended a compartmental stochastic model I implemented during my first internship (using C++)
    and performed a risk assessment analysis to study LA-MRSA colonization in french pork consumers.

  • April 2018 - May 2018 : Research intern, Conservatoire national des arts et métiers, Paris, France
    During this internship I Implementated a stochastic compartmental model of the spread of Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus Aureus (MRSA) in
    pig herds within french slaughterhouses (Using R).
